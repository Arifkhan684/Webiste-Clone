The quality of headphones encompasses several critical factors that enhance the listening experience. Sound quality is paramount, with a balanced audio profile that delivers clear highs, rich mids, and deep bass. Comfort is equally important; well-cushioned ear cups and adjustable headbands ensure a snug fit for extended wear.

Build quality plays a significant role as well, with durable materials contributing to longevity. Noise isolation capabilities, whether through passive cushioning or active noise cancellation, help to immerse the listener in their audio environment by blocking out distractions. For wireless models, battery life and connectivity are crucial, with longer battery durations and stable Bluetooth connections enhancing usability. Lastly, considering the brand's reputation can often guide consumers toward reliable options that deliver a satisfying performance relative to their price.
